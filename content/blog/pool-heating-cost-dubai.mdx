Most Dubai homeowners want to swim year-round, but pool water drops to 18–22°C in winter — too cold for comfortable use. Pool heating bridges that gap. Here's a clear comparison of the three options used in the UAE.

## Heat Pumps (Recommended for Most Dubai Pools)

Heat pumps extract ambient heat from outside air and transfer it to pool water. They use 1 unit of electricity to deliver 4–6 units of heat — the most efficient option in Dubai's climate.

**Why heat pumps work well in Dubai:**
- Ambient temperature rarely drops below 14°C (heat pump COP stays high)
- 365-day usability — pumps work efficiently in summer too (cooling mode available)
- Lower running cost than gas or electric resistance heating

**Sizing guide:**
- Small pool (up to 30 m²): 9–13 kW heat pump
- Medium pool (30–60 m²): 17–21 kW heat pump
- Large pool (60–100 m²): 26–35 kW heat pump
- Commercial pool (100+ m²): Multiple units or 50+ kW commercial heat pump

**Real cost example:** 50 m² villa pool in Dubai with 21 kW heat pump:
- Equipment + install: AED 35,000
- Monthly running cost (winter): AED 1,200
- Monthly running cost (summer maintenance): AED 400

## Gas Heaters

Gas heaters burn natural gas or LPG to heat water rapidly. They reach target temperature 3–4× faster than heat pumps.

**Pros:**
- Fastest heat-up time (useful for occasional weekend use)
- Performs in any weather
- Lower upfront cost than heat pumps

**Cons:**
- Highest running cost in Dubai (gas not always cost-effective)
- Requires gas line connection (often unavailable in Dubai villas)
- Combustion safety considerations
- Shorter lifespan than heat pumps

**Best for:** Commercial pools, hotels, resorts where rapid heat-up matters more than running cost.

---

## Electric Resistance Heaters

Electric resistance heaters use direct electrical resistance — like a giant kettle. Cheapest to install, most expensive to run.

**Pros:**
- Lowest upfront cost
- Compact size
- Simple installation

**Cons:**
- Highest running cost — 3–4× more expensive than heat pumps
- Slow heating
- High electricity demand (may require panel upgrade)

**Best for:** Small plunge pools, occasional-use spas, properties where heat pump installation is restricted.

## Insulation: The Hidden Heating Multiplier

A pool cover (thermal blanket) reduces heat loss by 50–75%. This means a 20 kW heat pump runs 60% less to maintain temperature.

**Cover types in Dubai:**
- Bubble solar cover: AED 800 – 3,000 (manual)
- Slatted automatic cover: AED 25,000 – 80,000 (motorised)
- Liquid pool cover: AED 200 – 400/month (chemical)

**ROI:** A AED 30,000 automatic cover pays back in heating savings within 4–5 years on a heated villa pool.

## Sizing Calculator (Rule of Thumb)

For Dubai outdoor pool with cover, target temperature 28°C:
- **Heat pump kW** = Pool surface area (m²) × 0.4

For uncovered Dubai outdoor pool:
- **Heat pump kW** = Pool surface area (m²) × 0.6

A 50 m² covered pool needs ~20 kW. Same pool uncovered needs ~30 kW.

## What Most Dubai Builders Get Wrong

**Undersizing:** Cheaper pumps that take 24+ hours to heat. Frustrating for users.

**No cover specified:** Heating without a cover wastes 60–70% of energy. Pool industry malpractice when not flagged.

**Wrong pump location:** Heat pumps need clear airflow. Cramped pump rooms reduce efficiency by 30%+.

**Missing automation:** Pumps running 24/7 even when pool is at temperature. Smart thermostats save AED 200–600 per month.
