Maintaining a swimming pool in Dubai isn't the same as maintaining one in Europe or North America. The heat, evaporation rate, dust, and salinity all create unique challenges. Here's a practical guide for UAE pool owners.

## Quick Maintenance Schedule

| Frequency | Tasks |
|-----------|-------|
| Daily (summer) | Check water level, skim debris |
| Weekly | Test pH, chlorine, alkalinity. Brush walls, vacuum |
| Monthly | Backwash filter, check equipment, deep clean tiles |
| Quarterly | Full chemistry test, equipment inspection |
| Annually | Drain partial, inspect shell, service heat pump |

---

## Why Dubai Pools Are Different

**Evaporation rates** in Dubai summer reach 12–18 mm/day. A 50 m² pool loses **600–900 litres per day** in July and August. This concentrates dissolved solids and pushes pH out of balance faster than cooler climates.

**Dust and sandstorms** deposit fine particles into pool water. After a major sandstorm, a pool needs additional vacuum and filtration cycles.

**High water hardness** in Dubai's tap water (typically 250+ ppm) leads to scale buildup on tiles and equipment if calcium hardness isn't managed.

**Year-round swim season** means constant chemical demand. Pools rarely "rest" the way they do in winter elsewhere.

---

## Critical Chemistry Levels

Dubai pools should be tested **weekly minimum** during summer:

| Parameter | Ideal Range | Why It Matters |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| pH | 7.2 – 7.6 | Comfort, equipment longevity |
| Free chlorine | 1 – 3 ppm | Sanitation |
| Total alkalinity | 80 – 120 ppm | pH stability |
| Calcium hardness | 200 – 400 ppm | Prevents scaling |
| Cyanuric acid | 30 – 50 ppm | UV protection for chlorine |
| TDS | < 1500 ppm above fill water | Water quality |

**Dubai-specific tip:** Cyanuric acid (chlorine stabiliser) is critical. Without it, UV destroys chlorine within hours. Indoor pools in Dubai don't need it; outdoor pools must have it.

---

## Summer Pool Care (May – September)

**1. Top up water daily.** Evaporation is severe. Auto-fill systems are worth the investment.

**2. Increase chlorine demand testing.** Heat accelerates chlorine consumption. You may need 1.5–2x your winter dosing.

**3. Run filtration longer.** Minimum 8–10 hours per day in summer. Variable-speed pumps allow this without huge electricity bills.

**4. Watch for algae after rare rain.** Even a 10-minute downpour can trigger algae if chlorine drops.

**5. Cover when not in use.** Pool covers reduce evaporation by up to 90% and chemical loss by 50%. Worth the investment.

---

## Winter Pool Care (October – April)

Most Dubai pools are still used year-round, but maintenance demand drops:

- Reduce filtration to 6 hours/day
- Test chemistry every 10 days (instead of weekly)
- Watch for cooler-water algae types (less common but possible)
- Service heat pump for upcoming summer use

---

## After a Sandstorm

Dust storms (haboobs) deposit fine particulate that overwhelms standard filtration. Recovery process:

1. Skim surface debris immediately
2. Brush walls and floor toward main drain
3. Vacuum to waste (not back through filter)
4. Backwash filter
5. Shock chlorinate (raise to 5–10 ppm)
6. Run filtration 24 hours
7. Test chemistry, rebalance

---

## Equipment Care in Dubai Heat

**Heat pumps** work harder in summer. Annual service prolongs life from 8–10 years to 12–15 years.

**Pumps** running continuously in 45°C+ ambient temps need ventilation. Pump rooms should have forced ventilation, not just passive vents.

**Filters** need backwashing every 3–4 weeks in summer (vs. monthly in winter).

**Salt cells** (for salt chlorination systems) need acid bath cleaning every 3 months due to harder water.
